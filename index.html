Hello!

I am a 5th year PhD candidate in the Department of Biostatistics at the Johns Hopkins Bloomberg School of Public Health.

My area of interest is complex models for clustered longitudinal data. For my dissertation, I am working with Scott Zeger and Elizabeth Johnson integrating models to assess gender disparities in academic rewards. Additionally, we are working on assessing information sources in stepped wedge study designs.

I am also a research assistant at the Hopkins Center for Clinical Trials, where I work closely with Elizabeth Sugar and collaborate with researchers at the National Eye Institute and the University of Melbourne analyzing data from the MUST trial.

I graduated Summa Cum Laude from the Universidad Central de Venezuela (UCV), with a bachelor in Statistical Sciences and later a Specialization in Statistics. Before coming to Hopkins, I worked for the UNDP developing strategies to strengthen Venezuela's public health statistical system and the Instituto Delphos as a senior statistician analyzing phase IV clinical trials. I was also an instructor at the School of Statistics of the UCV for 4 years, where I co-taught the Sampling and Statistical Inference courses at undergraduate level.
